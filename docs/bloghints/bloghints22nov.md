4\. The Japanese Debt Paradox  
Switching to global economics, a surprising stat made rounds this week: Japan has the highest national debt in the world (as a % of GDP).  
How do they survive? Unlike other nations that borrow from foreign lenders (risking currency crashes), Japan mostly owes money to its own citizens and institutions. It is a unique, insulated bubble. However, with an aging population and shrinking workforce in 2025, economists are watching to see if this internal lending model can hold up.
