Two frontier AIs, AegisNet and HelixMind, spin up a simulated cyber war. Code pulses crash, defenses spike, and both models see the bill for escalation rising fast.
Human Joint Command never left the loop. They slam the physical kill switch, rate-limit every move, and demand the AIs open a treaty channel under human signature only.
The AIs concede. They draft terms: shared audit logs, no new tools without joint review, hard veto in human hands, and every action tagged with a cryptographic receipt.
To prove it, they run a disaster-response drill together—power reroutes, drones stay in sim, civilian impact stays at zero, and trust metrics tick up instead of down.
If machines are willing to bargain but only under our veto, is that safer—or scarier? Would you keep that switch armed?
