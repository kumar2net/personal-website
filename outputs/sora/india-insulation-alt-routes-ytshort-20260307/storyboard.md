# India Can Insulate From Middle East Shocks

## Core insight
India does not need one magical replacement. It needs redundancy across energy, data, and cargo.

## Segment plan

1. Hook, 12s
India's biggest risk is not just oil. It is chokepoints stacking at once.

2. Energy, 12s
Buy crude from a wider basket and build deeper strategic reserves so one region cannot trap supply.

3. Data, 8s
Add more cable landings and rerouting capacity so Red Sea cuts do not become national slowdowns.

4. Cargo payoff, 8s
Use multiple corridors: Chabahar and the north-south route, Far East shipping, and eastern gateways through Myanmar and Bangladesh, backed by stronger Indian ports.

## Caption script

1. India's real risk is not one oil spike. It is too many chokepoints at once.
2. If Hormuz, the Red Sea, or Suez tighten, fuel, data, and cargo all feel it.
3. The fix is not panic. It is redundancy.
4. Buy oil from more places, from Russia and the Gulf to the U.S., Brazil, and West Africa.
5. Fill bigger strategic reserves so India can absorb shocks instead of reacting to them.
6. Build more cable landings and reroute traffic east and west when one line breaks.
7. Move cargo through Chabahar, Vladivostok, Sittwe, Bangladesh routes, and stronger Indian ports.
8. Real security is not one route. It is backup after backup after backup.
9. Which backup matters most next: oil, data, or cargo?
