Short version: the cortisol framework is useful, but the supplement framing needs more caution.
The strongest parts are the low-risk basics: light, exercise timing, food timing, and breathing.
My disagreement is narrow. Ashwagandha should not be normalized as a routine evening cortisol tool.
The evidence is not zero, but it is small, mixed, and paired with a real liver-safety warning.
So keep the framework, and treat ashwagandha as an optional footnote, not a default protocol.
