1. AI is not waiting only for smarter code. It is waiting for a bigger power plug.

2. The Kardashev scale is a civilization's power meter.

3. Today humanity sits around Type 0.73.

4. Type I sounds close, until you remember the scale is logarithmic.

5. That tiny jump means roughly 500 to 600 times more usable power.

6. So AI stops being only a model story. It becomes a grid story.

7. Training, inference, cooling, backup: all of it needs electricity.

8. IEA puts data centers at about 415 TWh in 2024.

9. Its base case points to roughly 945 TWh by 2030.

10. Data centers can rise in months. Substations and firm power do not.

11. Bitcoin's useful grid case is flexible demand, not magic.

12. The future may look like software; underneath it is turbines, transformers, copper, and cooling.
